Most discussions of software treat it as a product. There is a builder, a buyer, and a transaction. The software in between is a means to that transaction.

This framing leaves out a category I find myself increasingly interested in: software written by one person, used by one person, never sold, never marketed, never benchmarked against a competitor. Personal software. The dotfiles of the soul.

I have a handful of these. A small ledger I use to track expenses. A scraper that watches three websites I would otherwise check too often. A reader for the corner of the web I actually want to read. None of them would survive a code review. All of them are exactly what I need.
